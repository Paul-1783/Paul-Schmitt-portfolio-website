Whoami

I’ve worked over the past years in a variety of so-called odd jobs; among other things, I was a salesclerk in a bookstore in Pasing, Munich, encountering and interacting with people from all layers of German society. A job that gave me insight into the necessary mixture of patience as well as actual interest in and caring for co-workers and customers in a modern-day work environment.

I do consider myself, among other things, a bit of a tinker and a geek, so there was something missing in the aforementioned work life. I have already had some earlier experience with coding in a linguistic context and have always found it gratifying to see even the simplest of my programs actually work. As anybody who reads this can imagine, material reasons like good pay and a pleasant work place did factor into my decision to try to enter the web development world, but in terms of my attitude towards coding, I do hope for moments when I can actually rekindle that impression of gratification. I would like to think of myself as having the necessary combination of the qualities of maturity to be ready to face the daily routine of stressful programming work and also traces of the child-like passion for creating something through code.

Apart from sportive activities like jogging and half-marathons, in my spare time, I do have a passion for literature and languages. Constantly fluctuating between sound and superficial knowledge, like anybody who tries to master several languages at the same time, I never cease my attempts to improve. A continuing wish I don’t give up on is to publish one day a novel: The older you get, the smaller your dreams become, but, not all of them.
